# Hood Insulation Pad Replacement

## Overview
The original hood insulation pad (bonnet pad) on the 1991 Mercedes-Benz R129 has degraded and been completely removed by a previous owner. However, a significant amount of the original, hardened factory adhesive remains baked onto the underside of the aluminum hood. 

A new replacement kit (OE Number 1296802025, including pad, clips, test sticker, and spray adhesive) was ordered on March 22, 2026. This project covers the safe removal of the old adhesive from the aluminum hood and the installation of the new pad.

## Parts & Materials
* **Replacement Pad Kit:** IPG-87742-Set (OE 1296802025) - Includes pad, spray adhesive, fastening clips, and engine bay test sticker.
* **Adhesive Remover:** 3M Adhesive Remover, Goo Gone Automotive, or general Citrus-based solvent (do not use harsh acetones that will strip the paint).
* **Plastic Scrapers:** Trim removal tools or dedicated plastic razor blades. (NEVER use metal scrapers on the aluminum hood).
* **Protection:** Old blankets or drop cloths to cover the entire engine bay.
* **Masking Tape:** Wide painter's tape to protect painted edges.
* **Shop Vacuum:** Kärcher WD3 to catch falling debris.

## Action Plan

### Phase 1: Engine Bay Protection (CRITICAL)
- [ ] **1.1 — Cover the Engine Bay**
  - The old adhesive and remover fluid will make a massive, sticky mess. Cover the entire M119 engine, fenders, and windshield cowl with old blankets, thick plastic drop cloths, or heavy-duty garbage bags.
  - Tape the edges down so the wind/movement doesn't expose the engine.
- [ ] **1.2 — Mask Hood Edges**
  - Apply wide masking tape along the painted exterior edges of the hood to prevent adhesive remover from dripping onto the exterior clear coat.

### Phase 2: Old Adhesive Removal
*Note: The R129 hood is aluminum. It is soft and easily scratched or gouged. Use extreme patience.*
- [ ] **2.1 — Chemical Soak**
  - Work in small 30cm x 30cm sections. 
  - Spray or dab the adhesive remover onto the old hardened glue. 
  - Let it soak for 5-10 minutes. The old German adhesive is notoriously stubborn; the solvent must do the work, not your muscles.
- [ ] **2.2 — Plastic Scraping**
  - Using a wide plastic trim tool or plastic razor blade, gently scrape the softened adhesive away. 
  - Wipe the scraper clean on a shop towel immediately so you don't smear glue back onto the hood.
- [ ] **2.3 — Final Prep Wipe**
  - Once the bulk of the adhesive is gone, use a microfiber cloth soaked in brake cleaner or rubbing alcohol (Isopropyl) to wipe the aluminum completely clean and strip any remaining oily residue from the adhesive remover. 
  - *Pass criteria:* The underside of the hood must be 100% bare, clean, and dry, or the new spray adhesive will fail when the engine gets hot.

### Phase 3: Pad Installation
- [ ] **3.1 — Dry Fit**
  - Before spraying any glue, hold the new pad up to the hood and push the plastic fastening clips into their corresponding holes to align the pad perfectly. 
  - Take note of how it sits around the edges and hinges. 
  - Remove the clips and the pad.
- [ ] **3.2 — Apply Spray Adhesive**
  - Read the instructions on the provided spray adhesive (usually it requires application to BOTH the hood and the back of the pad, waiting 2-3 minutes for it to become "tacky" before joining).
  - Spray an even, medium coat on the aluminum hood and the back of the pad. Focus heavily on the perimeter edges.
- [ ] **3.3 — Mount and Press**
  - **Requires 2 people:** Carefully lift the pad into position. Do not let it touch the hood until it is perfectly aligned (contact cement grabs instantly).
  - Insert the plastic fastening clips first to anchor the pad in the correct location.
  - Starting from the center and working outward to the edges, press the pad firmly into the hood contour to ensure total adhesion.
- [ ] **3.4 — Curing**
  - Leave the hood open for at least 24 hours in a warm garage to allow the solvent gases to flash off and the glue to fully cure before starting the engine and introducing heat.
