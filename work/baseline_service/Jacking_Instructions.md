# Lifting the R129 Safely (Jack & Jack Stands)

The Mercedes-Benz R129 is a heavy car (approx. 1800–1900 kg) with a very stiff chassis. Safely lifting it requires the right equipment and knowledge of the specific factory lift points. **Never crawl under a car supported only by a hydraulic jack.**

## Equipment Needed (Available at Motonet/Kärkkäinen)
1. **Low-Profile Floor Jack (Hallitunkki):** Needs at least a 2-ton (2000kg) or 2.5-ton capacity. A "low profile" (matalaprofiilinen) jack is highly recommended to slide under the R129's side skirts.
2. **Jack Stands (Autopukit / Pukkisarja):** Buy 3-ton (3000kg) stands. Even though the car is 1.9 tons, you want the wider base and safety margin of a 3-ton stand. Pin-type stands or heavy-duty ratcheting stands are best.
3. **Rubber Jack Pads (Nostotyynyt / Tunkin kumi):** A thick rubber puck to place between your metal jack and the car's lifting points so you don't crack the plastic or scrape the paint.
4. **Wheel Chocks (Pyöräkiilat):** Essential to prevent the car from rolling while lifting.

## Identifying the Lift Points

The R129 has specific points designed to bear the weight of the vehicle:

- **Primary Side Lifting Points (For the Floor Jack):** Look under the side skirts, just behind the front wheels and just in front of the rear wheels. You will see rectangular rubber/plastic pads bolted to the chassis. These are the factory lift points.
- **Front Center Lift Point (Subframe):** There is a heavy crossmember under the engine. (Do NOT lift by the oil pan).
- **Rear Center Lift Point (Differential):** The flat bottom of the rear differential casing can be used to lift the entire rear end simultaneously.

## Step-by-Step Lifting Procedure (Front Axle)

1. **Prep the Car:** Park on a hard, perfectly flat surface (concrete or smooth asphalt). Put the transmission in Park and firmly apply the parking brake.
2. **Chock the Wheels:** Place wheel chocks firmly behind and in front of the **rear** tires.
3. **Position the Jack:** Slide your floor jack under the front-left rubber lift point. Place a rubber puck on the jack saddle.
4. **Lift:** Pump the jack until the front wheel is off the ground and there is enough room to slide a jack stand under the car.
5. **Place the Jack Stand:** Because the jack is occupying the factory rubber pad, place the jack stand under a solid, structural part of the front subframe or the heavy pivot point of the lower control arm (where it mounts to the chassis). **Do not place stands under floor pans or hollow sheet metal.**
6. **Lower Safely:** Slowly and gently release the jack pressure, letting the car settle onto the jack stand.
7. **Repeat:** Move to the front-right side and repeat the process. 
8. **Shake Test:** Once the front of the car is resting on two jack stands, give the front fender a firm shove. The car should be rock solid. If it rocks or shifts, lower it and reposition the stands.

## Working with ADS / SLS

Since your car has the Adaptive Damping System (ADS):
- Before removing any suspension arms or doing major chassis work, it is recommended to unbolt the tiny plastic **level sensor linkages** from the control arms so they don't snap when the suspension drops to full droop.
- For basic tasks like oil changes, fluid flushes, or electrical checks from underneath, the suspension can safely hang freely.

## Warning
- Never use the factory emergency scissor jack (in the trunk) for maintenance. It is highly unstable and only meant for emergency roadside tire changes. 
- Always ensure the jack stands are on a flat, hard surface. Asphalt can melt and sink in the summer; concrete is best.
