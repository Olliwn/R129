# Power Steering Fluid & Filter Flush

## Overview
The power steering fluid on the 1991 Mercedes-Benz 500 SL (R129) was observed to be below the minimum line and very dark/oxidized (March 17, 2026). Old fluid leads to premature wear of the steering pump and the expensive steering gear (recirculating ball box).

This project covers the "Turkey Baster" / syringe method of flushing the power steering fluid and replacing the internal paper filter, which is the safest and cleanest method for the M119 engine.

*Note for ADS cars:* The M119 in an ADS-equipped car uses a "Tandem Pump." The front half of the pump is for the power steering (uses MB 236.3 ATF, stored in the metal canister on the pump). The rear half of the pump is for the ADS/SLS suspension (uses ZH-M fluid, stored in a separate reservoir). **This guide is ONLY for the front Power Steering circuit.**

## Parts & Tools Needed
* **Fluid:** 1-2 Liters of MB 236.3 Spec Fluid (Febi 08972 purchased).
* **Filter:** Power Steering Filter Element (OE Part No. `A 000 466 21 04` or equivalent Mann/Mahle).
* **Tools:**
  * MTX 500ml Fluid Syringe / Extractor (purchased).
  * 10mm socket or wrench (to remove the filter hold-down nut).
  * Shop towels / rags (ATF is highly corrosive to paint; protect the engine bay).
  * Empty container for old fluid.

## Action Plan

### Phase 1: Initial Extraction & Filter Removal
- [ ] **1.1 — Protect the Area**
  - Place heavy shop towels or rags directly under and around the metal power steering reservoir (located front-left of the engine, behind the distributor).
- [ ] **1.2 — Extract Old Fluid**
  - Remove the reservoir cap (wing nut on top).
  - Use the fluid syringe to suck out as much of the old, dark fluid as possible from the reservoir. Empty it into your waste container.
- [ ] **1.3 — Remove the Old Filter**
  - Inside the reservoir, you will see a spring assembly holding down a paper filter cartridge.
  - Push down slightly on the spring to relieve tension, and use a 10mm socket/wrench to remove the central retaining nut.
  - Pull out the spring, the metal retaining plate, and use a pick tool or needle-nose pliers to fish out the old, sludgy paper filter.
- [ ] **1.4 — Deep Extraction**
  - With the filter out, use the syringe to suck out the remaining fluid at the very bottom of the canister.

### Phase 2: The "Turkey Baster" Flush
*Instead of disconnecting return lines and making a huge mess, we will cycle the fluid through the system manually.*
- [ ] **2.1 — First Refill**
  - **Do NOT install the new filter yet.**
  - Fill the reservoir up to the "MAX" line with fresh MB 236.3 fluid.
- [ ] **2.2 — Cycle the Steering (Engine OFF)**
  - Safely lift the front of the car so the front wheels are off the ground (see [Jacking Instructions](../baseline_service/Jacking_Instructions.md)).
  - Put the key in the ignition to unlock the steering wheel (do NOT start the engine).
  - Turn the steering wheel slowly from full lock-to-lock (all the way left, all the way right) about 5 to 10 times. This manually pumps the fresh fluid through the steering rack and pushes the old fluid back into the reservoir.
- [ ] **2.3 — Extract and Repeat**
  - Go back to the reservoir. The fluid should look dark again (mixed with the old fluid from the rack).
  - Syringe the fluid out again.
  - Refill with fresh fluid, and repeat the lock-to-lock steering cycles.
  - Repeat this process (usually 3 to 4 times) until the fluid you pull out of the reservoir looks bright red and clean.

### Phase 3: Final Assembly
- [ ] **3.1 — Install New Filter**
  - Drop the new paper filter (`A 000 466 21 04`) into the reservoir. It should seat firmly at the bottom.
  - Reinstall the metal plate, the spring, and tighten the 10mm retaining nut. (Do not overtighten; it is just holding a spring).
- [ ] **3.2 — Final Fill & Bleed**
  - Fill the reservoir to the correct level on the dipstick (attached to the cap). Note that there are usually cold (20°C) and hot (80°C) marks.
  - Lower the car to the ground.
  - Start the engine. The fluid level may drop immediately as the pump primes. Top up as necessary.
  - Turn the wheel lock-to-lock a few times with the engine running to bleed any remaining air.
  - Turn off the engine, let the foam/bubbles settle, and check the final level. Reinstall the cap tightly.
